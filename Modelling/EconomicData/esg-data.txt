EPC - EU Comparison:
https://eurodw.eu/revisiting-the-babel-tower-of-epc-ratings-updated-thresholds-across-europe/
> EPC ratings are highly inconsistent across Europe: Different countries (and even regions within countries) use varying methodologies, thresholds, and metrics, making cross-country comparisons unreliable.
> Methodologies and thresholds are evolving but not harmonised yet: Updates (e.g. France’s dual energy + CO₂ scale) and EU directives aim to standardise EPC calculations, but current data remains fragmented and difficult to source consistently.
> EPC data availability and quality remain a challenge: Data gaps are common in reporting (e.g. securitisation datasets), with some countries (like France and the Netherlands) showing better coverage than others.

EU Atlas project: 
https://www.epc-sri-atlas.eu/
> Centralised view of fragmented EPC landscape: The EPC Atlas aggregates 85+ EPC and Smart Readiness Indicator systems across EU Member States into a single interactive platform, helping users understand a highly fragmented market.
> Designed to improve comparability and transparency: The platform enables users to explore country-level EPC tools, methodologies, and systems, aiming to support standardisation and better cross-country comparison over time.
> Still evolving with gaps in coverage and depth: Some features (e.g. cost data, regulatory depth) are still developing, and the value of the tool depends on continuous updates and data completeness.

EU Building Passport (EUB SuperHub):
https://eubsuperhub.eu/e-passport
> EU is moving toward a “building passport” layer on top of EPCs: Combining EPC ratings with additional standardised KPIs, renovation pathways, and lifecycle data.
> Objective is to create a digital, comparable, finance-ready building dataset: Supporting decarbonisation, risk assessment, and cross-country transparency.
> It will not replace EPCs, but evolve them into “EPC 2.0”: With EPC remaining the regulatory base and passports adding depth, forward-looking insights, and usability.

Building Renovation Passport (iBRoad2EPC):
https://cordis.europa.eu/project/id/101033781
> Long-term renovation roadmap for a building
> Adds:
>> Step-by-step retrofit plan
>> Future regulatory requirements (e.g. fossil fuel bans)
>> Indicators like comfort, smart readiness, measured performance

Think: “EPC + forward-looking upgrade plan”
